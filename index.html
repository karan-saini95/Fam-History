Great-grandfather · Generation I
Rup Lal Trehan
c.1900 – 1975
Railway Engineer · British Colonial Service
Arya Samaj · Sri Hargobindpur, Punjab
1900s
Sri Hargobindpur, Gurdaspur district, Punjab
Born into the Khatri (Trehan clan) community in Punjab's Majha belt. The region has deep roots in Sikh history — Sri Hargobindpur is associated with the sixth Sikh Guru, and the Trehan lineage connects to Guru Angad, the second Guru. Rup Lal and his family were followers of Arya Samaj, believing in one formless God rather than idol or deity worship.
1920s
Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand)
After completing an engineering degree, follows his older brother — also a railway engineer — to Ranchi, then an important hub for the colonial railway network. The two brothers built their careers together within the British Indian Railways system.
c.1940
Limbe (Victoria), British Cameroons — West Africa
Following his older brother once again, posted to the coastal port town of Limbe during the Second World War as part of the British Empire's deployment of skilled engineers. His wife Lilawati accompanied him. Limbe played a role in port logistics and resource movement, linked administratively to Nigeria.
1941
A son born in Cameroon
Satya Paul Trehan is born in Limbe — the only member of the family line born outside India, on the West African coast.
1944–45
Return to India
The family returns to India when Satya Paul is around 3–4 years old, coinciding with the end of the Second World War and the wind-down of the imperial posting.
1975
Passed away
Rup Lal Trehan passed away in 1975, in his seventies. His wife Lilawati outlived him, passing in 1988.
Grandfather · Generation II
Major Satya Paul Trehan
1941 – 2010
Indian Army Officer — retired Major
Hindustan Motors · Lederle Labs
1941
Born in Limbe, British Cameroons
Born on the West African coast as a direct product of his father's imperial posting. Brought back to Punjab as a toddler, he would be raised in Ludhiana.
1950s
Ludhiana, Punjab
Raised in Ludhiana and attends Arya College, building on the family's tradition of education and public service.
1960s
Indian Military Academy, Dehradun
Commissioned as an officer through the Indian Military Academy in Dehradun, one of India's most prestigious military institutions.
1960s
Army postings — Delhi · Jalpaiguri, West Bengal · Jalandhar, Punjab
Serves approximately 8 years as an officer during India's nation-building phase. Postings span Delhi, Jalpaiguri (near the strategically vital Northeast Corridor), and finally Jalandhar — his last posting before retirement as a Major.
1972
Marriage in Ludhiana
Marries Narinder, from Fazilka — the match arranged by relatives on both sides. In keeping with the customs of the time, the two had not met before the wedding; she had only seen his photograph. The ceremony takes place in Ludhiana.
1970s
Calcutta, West Bengal
After retiring from the army, joins Hindustan Motors in a security and administrative role — one of India's major manufacturers, known for the Ambassador car. His daughter Ambika is born here in 1973.
1976
Valsad, Gujarat
Moves to Valsad to join Lederle Labs, part of the American Cyanamid Corporation — a shift from public-sector industry to a multinational pharmaceutical company.
1983
Faridabad, Haryana
Moves to Faridabad to work at another chemical company in the NCR's industrial belt, continuing his career in the private sector.
1985
Chandigarh — final home
The family settles in Chandigarh. His grandson Karan is born here a decade later and grows up in close proximity to him, absorbing his outlook, discipline, and breadth of experience.

Chandigarh · Panjab University
1973
Born in Fazilka, Punjab
Born at her maternal grandmother's home near the India–Pakistan border — a common practice of the time, returning to family for childbirth. The family is otherwise based in Calcutta.
1973–76
Calcutta, West Bengal
Early childhood in the city of her parents' professional life.
1976–83

1985 +
Chandigarh

Narinder Trehan's brother — a life in America
Karan's great-uncle on the maternal side · Los Angeles, California

One of Narinder Trehan's brothers (Karan's grandmother's sibling) made his way to the United States in the 1970s and settled in Beverly Hills, Los Angeles. He built a fortune in the denim industry and lived large — joining the prestigious Lions Club, marrying an American model, and later remarrying another. He had a large mansion there, which he named Taj Mahal. By the accounts of those who knew him, he was a man of considerable exuberance and appetite for life.

The fortune, however, did not hold. Legal troubles mounted, and when a court pronounced him guilty in a lawsuit, he shot himself in the courtroom.

His story is one of the more dramatic threads in the family's history — a man who crossed the same ocean as Karan would, decades earlier, reached Beverly Hills from a Punjabi railway town, and met an end as vivid as his ambitions.

Narinder Trehan's father — Permanent Way Engineer, Northern Indian Railways
Karan's great-grandfather on the maternal side · Doaba region, Punjab

Narinder Trehan's father (Karan's maternal great-grandfather) was a Class I officer in the Northern Indian Railways, serving in the Permanent Way department — the branch responsible for laying, inspecting, and maintaining rail tracks. It was demanding, field-based engineering work that required postings across large stretches of territory.

Originally from the Doaba region of Punjab — the fertile belt between the Beas and Sutlej rivers — he was posted across northern India over the course of his career, including Kapurthala, Firozpur, and Jalalabad in Punjab. His railway career placed him in the same broad institutional world as Rup Lal Trehan on the other side of the family, though through an entirely different branch of the service.

The railway connection on both sides of Karan's family — Rup Lal as an engineer in British colonial service, and Narinder's father as a Class I Permanent Way officer in the Northern Indian Railways — is a quiet coincidence that threads through the family's history.
